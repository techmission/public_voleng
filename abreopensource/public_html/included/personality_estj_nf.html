ESTJs are practical, realistic, and matter-of-fact, with a natural head for business or mechanics. Though they are not interested in subjects they see no use for, they can apply themselves when necessary. An ESTJ looks for facts first and value the concrete first, but they do have a keen introverted sensing ability.

ESTJs have a strong sense of ethical standards and beliefs that they live by. This makes them honest and candid individuals, but can also inadvertently make them come off as coarse. The reality is that an ESTJ sees the world in terms of social order and will say and do what they have to do to maintain and promote those ideals. However, a SJ (Sensing and Judging) temperament is one who is always ready to give rather than receive. They also have a great respect for elders and long held traditions.

They like to organize and run activities. They are natural leaders who feel most comfortable when they are in charge of a outreach or a task force within an outreach. ESTJs make good administrators, especially if they remember to consider others' feelings and points of view, which they often miss. ESTJs use their gifts to take very logical approaches to tackling problems and they look for tangible results. Within an outreach, the ST (Sensing and Thinking) gifts make them likely to be the most willing to take on any task that they are needed to accomplish. Shutting out distractions, they take a practical, logical approach to their endeavors. Realistic and responsible, they work steadily toward their goals. The gifts of an ST allow them to remember experiences and organize them and apply them to a current challenge, which can keep an outreach that an ESTJ is in from making critical mistakes.
