The Guardian

ESTJs are practical, realistic, and matter-of-fact, with a natural head for business or mechanics. Though they are not interested in subjects they see no use for, they can apply themselves when necessary. An ESTJ looks for facts first and value the concrete first, but they do have a keen introverted sensing ability.

ESTJs have a strong sense of ethical standards and beliefs that they live by. This makes them honest and candid individuals, but can also inadvertently make them come of as coarse. The reality is that an ESTJ sees the world in terms of social order and will say and do what they have to do to maintain and promote those ideals. However, a SJ (Sensing and Judging) temperament is one who is always ready to give rather than receive. They hear Christ's call to care for the needy in especially practical ways. They also enjoy the ritual and ceremony of a faith filled life and have a great respect for elders of the church and long held traditions.

They like to organize and run activities. They are natural leaders who feel most comfortable when they are in charge of a ministry or a task force within a ministry or church. ESTJs make good administrators, especially if they remember to consider others' feelings and points of view, which they often miss. ESTJs use their gifts to take very logical approaches to tackling problems and they look for tangible results. Within a ministry, the ST (Sensing and Thinking) gifts make them likely to be the most willing to take on any task that they are needed to accomplish. Shutting out distractions, they take a practical, logical approach to their endeavors. Realistic and responsible, they work steadily toward their goals. The gifts of an ST allow them to remember experiences and organize them and apply them to a current challenge, which can keep a ministry that an ESTJ is in from making critical mistakes.

ESTJs are likely to enjoy ministry areas and callings where they can:
Work in a systematic and organized fashion
Contribute to straightforward assignments using reason
Feel as though their goals and expections are practical and reasonable
Be in a friendly environment with other hardworking people
Manage others using a fair and objective system
Serve in a stable and predictable environment
Serve alongside a variety of people
Set and meet deadlines
Your Ministry Strengths May Include:
Staying focused on a ministry's goals
Ability to be tough when necessary
Practical approach to problem solving
Precision in getting a project done
Organizational skills
Ability to work within a structure
Giftedness in identifying illogical aspects of a ministry's plan
Work ethic with a value on efficiency
Your Ministry Weaknesses May Include:
Impatience with those who don't work well within structures
Reluctance to try on new ideas
Ignoring the future to focus on the present
Impatience with inefficient processes
Diffuclty listening to the viewpoints of others
Tendency to overlook how policies affect others
